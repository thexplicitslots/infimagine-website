Material guide

Choose by use, not by name.

Materials behave differently under load, heat, UV, friction, and finishing. We recommend the print route after we understand the job.

PLA Best for clean visual prints, decor, display pieces, models, and gifts.

Sharp detail, lower heat resistance, great for aesthetic objects.

PETG Best for stronger everyday parts, brackets, mounts, organizers, and light functional use.

Tougher than PLA, better impact behavior, practical for many utility pieces.

ABS / ASA Best for heat-facing, outdoor, enclosure, and more durable functional projects.

More demanding to print, useful when temperature or weather matters.

Nylon Best for high endurance parts, clips, hinges, load paths, and repeated-use components.

Strong and resilient, often chosen when durability matters more than a perfect visual finish.

PEEK Best for extreme endurance engineering contexts where heat and chemical resistance matter.

Specialty high-performance material, quoted carefully based on geometry and requirement.

Flexible Best for grips, cushions, sleeves, soft-touch elements, and parts that need controlled flex.

Useful when the object must bend, absorb, protect, or feel softer in hand.

Selection logic

A better material choice starts with questions.

Environment

Where will it live?

Indoor display, outdoor exposure, heat, sunlight, moisture, and handling all change the best material.

Force

What stress will it take?

Compression, bending, impact, clips, screw loads, and repeated motion affect material and orientation.

Finish

How should it look?

Visual parts may prioritize surface quality, color, and clean geometry over maximum strength.

Precision

Does it need to fit?

Functional parts need tolerance planning, measuring references, and sometimes test iterations.

By project type

Match the material to the brief.

The same object can be printed in different ways depending on whether it is a gift, prototype, model, or functional part.

Custom 3D printing Prototype printing Personalized gifts
Gifts

Prioritize finish and presence

PLA, PETG, matte colors, scale, base design, and surface cleanup often matter more than engineering strength.

Prototypes

Prioritize the test

Use PLA for fast form checks, PETG or ABS/ASA for more realistic testing, and Nylon for tougher parts.

Utility

Prioritize reliability

Brackets, mounts, adapters, and replacement parts need load, heat, and fit details before material selection.

Specialty

Prioritize constraints

PEEK and high-endurance routes are treated as engineering quotes because the print conditions and geometry matter deeply.
